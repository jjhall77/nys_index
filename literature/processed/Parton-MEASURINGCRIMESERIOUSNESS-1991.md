The role of victim harm

The role of victim harm

Challenges to the assumption that crime seriousness judgements are judgements of victim harm take two general forms. First, there are multidimensional scaling studies which purportedly reveal dimensional attributes of the crime vignettes used by the respondents. The work of Forgas (1980) is of particular interest because he used crime vignettes consisting of summary labels (e.g., assault, prostitution, and gambling). Such crime vignettes provide the respondent with no information about the offender's intentions. Nevertheless, the fourth dimension extracted in his analysis he labelled 'intention'. The obvious conclusion is that respondents hold crime stereotypes which include views about the offender's culpability. Consistent with Forgas, Rahav (1980) concluded that intentionality and victim harm were the two primary dimensions represented in the offences sorted by the respondents.
